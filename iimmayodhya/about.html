history of IIMM ayodhya

Based on search results, there is no evidence of an Indian Institute of Management (IIM) in Ayodhya. However, the Indian Institute of Materials Management (IIMM) established a new branch there in 2024.

Here is a summary of the history for both organizations:

Indian Institute of Materials Management (IIMM) in Ayodhya

  • Establishment: In July 2024, the Indian Institute of Materials Management (IIMM) held a meeting in Ayodhya to establish a new local branch.
  • Initial success: The new Ayodhya branch was inaugurated with an impressive 111 paid life members, an unprecedented number for the IIMM.
  • National seminar: The inauguration was accompanied by a national seminar on "Supply Chain Management: Basic-2-Robotic," with approximately 200 delegates in attendance.
  • Future potential: The IIMM recognized Ayodhya as an area with significant growth potential, providing an excellent opportunity for the organization to establish a stronger foothold.

Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs)

Ayodhya is not home to an IIM. The closest and fourth-oldest IIM is located in the neighboring city of Lucknow.

  • Founding: The Indian Institutes of Management were established by the Government of India, following a vision set forth by the country's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru. IIM Lucknow:
  • The Indian Institute of Management, Lucknow (IIML), was established in 1984. It is situated on a 200-acre campus in Lucknow, not Ayodhya.
  • Other campuses: The IIM system has expanded to include many institutes across India, including a satellite campus for IIM Lucknow in Noida.

vision and mission of IIMM

The vision of the Indian Institute of Materials Management (IIMM) is to elevate the profession of materials and supply chain management by developing professional standards and skills across various industries.

IIMM's vision

IIMM's vision is focused on being a national apex body that sets the standard for the materials and supply chain management profession in India. Key elements of their vision include:

  • Wider recognition: Gaining broader recognition and promoting the importance of efficient materials management in all types of commercial and industrial enterprises, both in India and abroad.
  • Globally aligned curriculum: Creating programs and standards that are in line with international benchmarks, particularly those set by the International Federation of Purchasing and Supply Management (IFPSM).
  • Professional elevation: Improving the general and professional knowledge of management by sharing information and encouraging thought leadership in materials-related functions like procurement, manufacturing, logistics, and supply chain.

IIMM's mission

To achieve its vision, IIMM's mission involves a range of activities and objectives aimed at equipping professionals with the knowledge and skills they need to excel.

Key mission objectives include:

  • Professional development: Constantly imparting advanced professional knowledge to improve the skills of individuals working in the materials management field.
  • Educational offerings: Developing and offering a curriculum of study and training, including diplomas, certificates, and post-graduate programs in materials management and supply chain.
  • Knowledge dissemination: Organizing lectures, conferences, seminars, and other events to provide information on management practices.
  • Ethical standards: Promoting and enforcing a strict code of conduct and ethics among its members to ensure professional integrity.
  • Networking: Fostering professional and friendly relationships among members who are engaged in materials management.
